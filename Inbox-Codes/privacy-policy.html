Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 2025

This Privacy Policy applies to the CodeLift browser extension (formerly "Email Code Extractor"). We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting it. This extension was built with a "privacy-first" architecture, meaning your data stays on your device.

1. Data Collection

CodeLift does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data.

The extension operates entirely locally within your browser. It scans the visible text of your Gmail inbox solely for the purpose of identifying numeric verification patterns (such as "Code: 123456"). This processing happens in your computer's Random Access Memory (RAM) and is discarded immediately after the tab is closed or refreshed.

  • We do not have servers.
  • We do not use analytics or tracking cookies.
  • We do not read your emails other than scanning the inbox subject/snippet lines for numeric codes.

2. Permissions Usage

To function correctly, CodeLift requires specific browser permissions. Here is how we use them:

  • Host Permission (mail.google.com): Required to inject the script that identifies codes and adds the "Copy" button to your inbox rows.
  • ClipboardWrite: Required to copy the detected code to your clipboard when (and only when) you click the extension icon.

3. Third-Party Services

This extension does not integrate with any third-party APIs, ad networks, or data brokers.
